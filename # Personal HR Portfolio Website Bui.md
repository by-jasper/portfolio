# Personal HR Portfolio Website Build Brief

## 1. Website Purpose

Build a personal portfolio website for an HR professional based in Singapore.

The website should serve two purposes:

1. Recruiter-facing portfolio  
   - Showcase work experience
   - Highlight HR operations, payroll, HR systems, process improvement, automation, and AI interest
   - Encourage recruiters to reach out for job opportunities

2. HR professional information portal  
   - Share useful HR operations knowledge
   - Share automation ideas
   - Share responsible AI use cases for HR
   - Position the owner as a practical and forward-looking HR professional

The website should not feel like a normal resume only. It should feel like a professional career portfolio plus HR knowledge hub.

---

## 2. Target Audience

Primary audience:
- Recruiters
- Hiring managers
- HR leaders
- HR Shared Services leaders
- Payroll managers
- HRIS / People Operations teams

Secondary audience:
- HR professionals
- HR operations practitioners
- HR automation learners
- HR professionals interested in AI

---

## 3. Personal Positioning

The website should position the owner as:

> HR Operations & Payroll Professional with strong interest in AI, automation, HR systems, and process improvement.

The key message:

> I am not only an HR operations person. I am an HR professional who understands payroll, compliance, systems, process improvement, and how AI can support the future of HR work.

---

### Case Study 1: Payroll Error Prevention and Recovery Process

Title:

> Reducing Payroll Error Risk Through Better Tracking and Controls

Problem:

> Payroll errors, late amendments, or unclear follow-up can create employee impact, rework, and recovery issues.

My Role:

> Supported issue review, stakeholder coordination, correction tracking, and employee communication.

Actions Taken:

- Reviewed payroll-related issues and clarified impact
- Coordinated with relevant stakeholders and vendors
- Prepared clear communication for affected employees or HR partners
- Supported tracking of correction status and follow-up actions
- Helped identify areas where clearer controls or checks were needed

Result / Value:

> Improved visibility of payroll issues, supported timely follow-up, and helped reduce confusion during correction or recovery processes.

Skills Used:

- Payroll operations
- Data checking
- Stakeholder coordination
- Employee communication
- Process control

---

### Case Study 2: HR Query Management and Tracking

Title:

> Structuring HR Query Handling for Better Follow-Up

Problem:

> Employee HR queries can come from multiple channels, making it difficult to track ownership, response status, and recurring issues.

My Role:

> Explored ways to structure query intake, tracking, and follow-up using simple digital tools.

Actions Taken:

- Reviewed common types of HR queries
- Suggested structured intake using forms or standard templates
- Explored SharePoint List / Microsoft Forms / workflow-based tracking
- Considered status fields, ownership, response timelines, and reporting views
- Improved clarity in employee-facing responses

Result / Value:

> Created a clearer approach for managing HR queries, improving visibility, accountability, and response consistency.

Skills Used:

- HR Shared Services
- Employee service
- Process design
- Microsoft Forms
- SharePoint Lists
- Power Automate concept

---

### Case Study 3: HR System Migration Support

Title:

> Supporting HR System Migration from an Operations Perspective

Problem:

> HR system migration can create uncertainty in payroll, attendance, absence, approvals, and employee query handling.

My Role:

> Supported process review, communication, issue clarification, and operational readiness.

Actions Taken:

- Reviewed system behaviour from payroll and attendance perspective
- Helped clarify payroll cut-off and absence impact
- Prepared communication to explain system-related delays or changes
- Identified process gaps and escalation points
- Coordinated with stakeholders to support smoother transition

Result / Value:

> Helped reduce confusion, supported better understanding of system behaviour, and improved communication during transition.

Skills Used:

- HR systems
- Payroll operations
- Change support
- Communication drafting
- Process review

---

### Case Study 4: Work Pass Renewal and Compliance Tracking

Title:

> Improving Work Pass Renewal Visibility and Follow-Up

> Work pass renewal requires accurate tracking, timely documents, business input, and follow-up to avoid last-minute issues.

My Role:

> Supported tracking, documentation, communication, and follow-up for renewal-related matters.

Actions Taken:

- Tracked renewal timelines and required actions
- Prepared clear communication to employees and stakeholders
- Followed up on missing information
- Supported approval or waiver-related documentation where required
- Maintained attention to compliance and business continuity

Result / Value:

> Improved visibility of renewal status and reduced risk of missed follow-up.

Skills Used:

- Work pass administration

- Stakeholder follow-up
- Documentation

### Case Study 5: AI-Assisted HR Automation and VBA Generation

Title:

> Using AI to Support HR Automation While Protecting Confidential Data

Problem:

> HR teams often spend significant time on repetitive tasks such as document generation, email drafting, report formatting, and data checking.

My Role:

> Explored how AI can support automation logic and VBA generation while respecting internal control and data protection requirements.

Actions Taken:

- Used AI to generate sample VBA logic for HR-related workflows
- Used dummy or non-sensitive data when testing ideas
- Refined scripts for document generation, email templates, and Excel automation
- Avoided uploading confidential employee information into AI tools
- Reviewed outputs manually before applying them in work context

Result / Value:

> Demonstrated how AI can speed up automation development while keeping HR data protection and human review as key controls.

Skills Used:

- AI prompting
- VBA logic
- Excel automation
- HR document generation

Use short paragraphs.

Good style:

> I supported HR operations across payroll, employee queries, benefits, leave administration, and work pass matters. My work focuses on accuracy, service quality, process improvement, and clear communication.

Avoid:

> I am a hardworking and passionate individual who always gives 100%.

- Improved
- Reviewed
- Coordinated
- Structured
- Automated
- Explored

- Documented
